Steam feature design

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

This project was individual and took place over three weeks. The goal was to develop a new feature for a mobile application out of a pool of 10 apps, for my project I chose Steam mobile.

First steps

Before doing any sketching or prototyping I conducted research on my own by doing short interviews with two current users of the app to get a feel for what the app is for, what features they use most often, and areas where it could be improved. The two users and their most used features were figure 1 which is the market listing page and figure 2 is the library feature. I also had to familiarize myself with the design language of Steam mobile because one of the objectives was to have our feature integrate as seamlessly as possible into the existing interface.

For the second section of the project I had to sketch wireframes for two potential features to lay out where they would go and what it would look like. One of which involved a follow button pictured in figure 3 and 4 in the broadcasts section of the app, and a select all button shown in figure 5 for when users confirm their Steam market listings.

Eventually I found that the broadcasting section of the app was a greater area for exploration because there were more ways the problem could be addressed in contrast to the market listing solution that could be fixed with one button.

In the final stages of the project I used the prototyping tool UXpin to create my design. The biggest challenge I faced was finding a way to expand upon my follow button in figure 7 because the solution was not that complex. I did this by giving the user more options to easily search for streams they might want to watch by introducing a set of filters as seen in figure 6, that they can toggle on and off. When a user finds a stream they like and follow, they can also access a tab shown in figure 8, so that they know when a streamer is online in the future.
